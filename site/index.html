Vocabulary for Museums

  • a curator – a person who oversees and manages a museum and its collections
    To become a curator of a museum , you need to work at public service for years.
  • a tour guide – a person employed to show tourists around places of interest
    When I was in Rome in 2015 , there were plenty of groups with their tour guides . Most of them were from far east countries.
  • preservation – to maintain something in its original or existing state
    Turkey must behave more professional about preservation of the ancient remains.
  • to wander (around) – to walk slowly across or around an area, usually without clear purpose
    The best way to see a new city is wandering around. This way you will find the places of interest by yourself.(on your own)
  • a collection – a group of accumulated paintings, documents, or artefacts grouped together by a particular theme
    In the leonardo da vinci museum , you can find a collection of diffierent professions ; engineering , science , art.
  • an artefact – an object made by a human being, of cultural or historical interest
    The artefacts found in Turkey are being exhibited in several countries.
  • an admission fee – the fee charged to enter a place.
    A tourist would pay 4-5 euros for admission to enter the buildings of interest.
  • an exhibition – a public display of works of art or items of interest held in a museum or gallery for people to see
    It is a thrilling experience to visit the exhibitions in istanbul.

Vocabulary for Work

  • to be called for an interview: to be invited to attend an interview
    Yesterday, I received a call. An automative company called me for an inverview.
  • to be your own boss: to have your own business
    I would like to quit my job and be my own boss .
  • a dead-end job: a job with no promotional opportunities
    People must create own opportunities instead of working at a dead-end job.
  • to do a job-share: to share the weekly hours of work with another person
    It is common to teachers to job-share.
  • a heavy workload: to have a lot of work to do
    In the lift industry Everybody has a heavy workload.
  • a high-powered job: an important or powerful job
    People who are working in executive positions at international companies have high-powered jobs.
  • manual work: work that requires physical activity
    People who work in construction jobs do a lot of manual work.
  • maternity leave: time off work given to a woman about to have a baby
    In some developed countries, Not only women but also Men have maternity leave rights which is called paternity.
  • to meet a deadline: to finish a job by an agreed time
    Meeting deadlines is the most important thing for Japanese Automative Companies.
  • a nine-to-five job: a normal job that consists of an 8 hour day (approximately)
    Majority of people are working at a nine-to-five job which is quite boring. Having flexible working hours must be great.
  • to take early retirement: to retire early (retire: to reach an age when you are allowed to stop working for a living)
    In Turkey, the age of being eligible for retirement is 65. It is almost impossible to take early retirement.
  • to be stuck behind a desk: to be unhappy in an office job
    I want to change my work because I feel stuck behind my desk .
  • sick leave: time allowed off work when sick
    My colleague fills in for me when I take sick leave.

Vocabulary for Animals

  • Adaptation — genetically determined specialization of an animal’s physical structure or behavior which helps the animal survive
    Wild animals are struggling with adapting to the global warming
  • Defenses — the methods or characteristics a living thing has to protect itself
    Every animal has their own defence mechanism to protect themselves.
  • Evolve — to change over a long period of time, through many generations
    Hands evolved over millions of years.This brought mankind a unique ability to make tools.
  • Temperate — having a climate which does not get extremely hot or cold
    Mammals like a temperate climate.
  • Domesticated – living in a house with people (such as pets)
    Domesticated animals have become best friends of humans in the quarantine lockdowns
  • Extinct — plant or animal species that no longer exists
    Magellanic Penguins are becoming extinct because of weather conditions
  • Fur – animal hair
    Most animals are being hunted for their fur by humans
  • Feathers – long special hair on a bird
    Making pillows by using bird feathers was/is a common tradition.
  • Hooves – animal feet, like horses and goats
    The horses left hoove marks on the mud
  • Enclosure — the man-made habitat of an animal in captivity
    Enclosured areas are very important to keep animals that are becoming extinct safe.
  • Predator — an animal which must hunt and kill other animals for food
    Eagles are very dangerous predators
  • Detritivore — animals that feed on dead plant and animal matter
    Vultures and Hyenas are detritivores.
  • Diurnal — active by day
    Most animals are diurnal
  • Nocturnal — performing most actions (feeding, breeding, etc.) at night
    Most owls and bats are nocturnal creatures.
  • Mammals — a class of warm-blooded, usually hairy animals, that feed their young with milk secreted by the mammary glands of females
    Mankind are mammals.
  • Reptiles – a class of animals that are cold-blooded, with scales
    Snakes are cold-blooded reptiles.
  • Amphibians – a class of animals that can live in the water and on land, no scales
    Crocodiles are amphibians
